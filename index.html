Mercy Between Hearts

Day 1
The Purpose of Marriage in Islam

In Islam, marriage is a sacred covenant (mithaqun ghalithun) between two people. It's not just a physical or emotional union, but a spiritual bond with the purpose of:

• Achieving tranquility and peace

• Fulfilling half of your faith

• Procreating and raising righteous children

• Protecting one's chastity and fulfilling desires in a halal way

Day 2
Rights and Responsibilities

Islam clearly defines the rights and responsibilities of both spouses to create balance in marriage:

• Mutual respect and kindness

• The wife's right to financial support

• The husband's right to be obeyed in what is right

• Mutual consultation in family matters

• Education and protection of children

Completed
Day 3
Communication in Islamic Marriage

Effective communication is essential for a successful marriage. Islamic teachings emphasize:

• Speaking with kindness and respect

• Avoiding harsh words and criticism

• Practicing active listening

• Resolving disputes privately and with wisdom

• The importance of smiling and gentle speech

Day 4
Financial Wisdom in Marriage

Islam provides guidance on financial matters within marriage:

• The husband's responsibility to provide

• The wife's right to her own wealth

• Avoiding extravagance and waste

• Giving charity regularly

• Planning for the family's future

• Transparency in financial matters

Day 5
Spiritual Intimacy

Building spiritual connection strengthens marital bonds:

• Praying together when possible

• Reading and studying Quran together

• Fasting together occasionally

• Making dua for each other

• Attending Islamic lectures and events together

• Creating an Islamic environment at home

Day 6
Conflict Resolution

Islam provides guidance for resolving marital disputes:

• Seeking mediation when needed

• Avoiding anger and harsh words

• Practicing patience and forgiveness

• Remembering the temporary nature of disputes

• Focusing on solutions rather than blame

• Making up after arguments
